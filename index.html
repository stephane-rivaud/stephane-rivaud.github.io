Growth strategies for arbitrary DAG neural architectures

Douka S., Verbockhaven M., Rudkiewicz T., Rivaud S., Landes F., Chevallier S., Charpiat G.

ESANN 2025 — European Symposium on Artificial Neural Networks

Neural Architecture DAG Networks Growth Strategies
Paper 2

We present novel strategies for growing arbitrary directed acyclic graph (DAG) neural architectures during training. Our approach enables dynamic adaptation of network topology based on functional gradient descent, achieving significant computational savings compared to traditional neural architecture search methods.

PETRA: Parallel End-to-end Training with Reversible Architectures

Rivaud S., Fournier L., Pumir T., Belilovsky E., Eickenberg M., Oyallon E.

ICLR 2025 — International Conference on Learning Representations

Distributed Training Reversible Networks Parallel Computing
Paper 10

We introduce PETRA, a novel framework for parallel end-to-end training of deep neural networks using reversible architectures. Our method enables efficient model parallelism without the memory overhead of traditional approaches, achieving up to 3× speed-up on large-scale models while maintaining comparable accuracy.

2023

Can forward gradient match backpropagation?

Fournier L., Rivaud S., Belilovsky E., Eickenberg M., Oyallon E.

ICML 2023 — International Conference on Machine Learning

Optimization Forward Gradient Backpropagation
Paper 12

We investigate whether forward gradient methods can achieve comparable performance to backpropagation in training deep neural networks. Our theoretical and empirical analysis reveals conditions under which forward gradients can match or exceed backpropagation efficiency.
